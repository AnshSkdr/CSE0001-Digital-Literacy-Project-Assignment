A common situation where poor digital communication can cause problems is when a student sends an unprofessional email to a professor. For example, writing a message without a proper subject line, using informal language like “hey” or “send deadline pls,” or not clearly explaining the request can create a negative impression. In some cases, the professor may ignore the email or misunderstand the request, leading to missed deadlines or loss of marks.

Such issues can be avoided by following basic email etiquette. The student should use a clear subject line, begin with a respectful greeting, and write the message in a polite and structured manner. It is also important to clearly state the purpose of the email and include necessary details. Ending with a proper sign-off adds professionalism. Good digital communication ensures clarity, respect, and better outcomes in academic and professional interactions.
