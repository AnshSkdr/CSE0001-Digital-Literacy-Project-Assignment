# 📄 Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime where attackers trick users into transferring money through digital payment apps. With the rapid growth of online payments in India, such frauds have increased significantly.

A real incident reported in India involved a man from Delhi who lost around ₹20 lakh after his mobile phone was stolen. The fraudsters accessed his banking apps and carried out unauthorized UPI transactions from his account. The case revealed that cybercriminals often exploit lost or stolen devices to gain access to sensitive financial information and transfer money instantly. ([The Times of India][1])

Typically, UPI fraud begins when attackers contact victims via calls, messages, or fake apps, pretending to be bank officials or service providers. They create urgency and convince victims to share OTPs, click malicious links, or approve “collect requests.” Once the victim complies, money is transferred immediately.

Students, elderly individuals, and people unfamiliar with digital security are common targets. The consequences include financial loss, emotional stress, and reduced trust in digital systems. In many cases, recovering the money is difficult.

This case highlights the importance of digital awareness, such as not sharing sensitive details, securing devices, and verifying transactions before approval.
